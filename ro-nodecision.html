The Champion Problem
Your champion wanted it. That wasn't enough.

There's a specific deal archetype that shows up constantly. It has all the right signals early on — multiple conversations, genuine enthusiasm, a clear problem, someone inside the organisation who clearly gets it. And then it slows down.

The champion is still responsive. Still positive. Still says it's moving. But meetings get harder to schedule. Replies take longer. Nothing is explicitly wrong — but nothing is happening either.

The pattern, almost every time: the champion wanted the solution but wasn't responsible for making it happen. They had influence, not ownership. And at some point — usually around the time budget or procurement entered the picture — the decision moved to someone who was never in the room with you.

The tell: when you ask "what does the next step look like?", the champion describes a process rather than naming a person. Process answers mean no one owns the decision yet. That's the moment to stop pushing and start asking different questions.
