Why me

I lost a deal exactly like yours.

It looked completely real. Multiple strong conversations. An engaged champion. A clear problem. The feeling that it was just a matter of time. Then it slowed down. Replies got shorter. Meetings got harder to schedule. I kept pushing forward, more follow-ups, more information, more attempts to create next steps.

What I didn't know was that I was speaking to someone who wanted it but wasn't responsible for making it happen. By the time I figured that out, the deal was already over, not formally lost, just finished. What it cost me wasn't just the deal. It was the weeks of time, attention, and false confidence in a pipeline number that wasn't real.

That experience taught me to look at one specific thing: whether decision ownership actually exists on the buyer's side, and where it disappeared. That's the pattern I've been paying attention to ever since and it's the one thing that most deal reviews never surface.

Common questions

What people ask before they book.

Who is this for exactly?
Founders personally running a high-value B2B deal who can't get a straight answer on where it stands. Not sales leaders managing a team. Not early-stage deals without a pattern.
What counts as a "high-value" deal?
The number that makes 90 minutes worth it. Usually a deal that changes your quarter or your year. No fixed threshold — but if the outcome either way wouldn't change much, this probably isn't the right tool.
What do I need to bring to the session?
Nothing prepared in advance. Just the deal — the conversations, the people, where it was tracking and when it changed. Minimum three or four real interactions with the buyer. If it's too early to have a pattern, the session won't work yet.
What if the verdict is that the deal is already over?
Then that's what I'll tell you. That's exactly what this session is for — and it's the outcome that most founders are actually afraid to find out. Knowing the deal is no longer active isn't a failure. It's the end of weeks of false confidence, misallocated attention, and a forecast number that isn't real. A clear no is worth more than six more months of drift.
How is this different from a sales consultant or advisor?
I don't build pipelines, redesign sales processes, or offer general growth advice. I work inside one live deal at the moment it matters most. I'm listening for one specific pattern that explains almost every stalled deal I've looked at: whether decision ownership exists on the buyer's side and where it disappeared. That's a narrow focus, and it's intentional.
What's the refund policy?
If you go through the session and don't feel you got a clear verdict, tell me. I'll either extend the time or refund it. I'm not interested in charging for ambiguity — that's the problem you came with.
What happens after Phase 1 if I want to keep going?
If the deal has a path forward and you want to execute the move rather than figure it out alone, that's Phase 2. We work the deal together twice a week until it reaches a real decision — closed or clearly over. Phase 2 is $5,000 and starts directly from the verdict session. If it doesn't, Phase 2 doesn't apply.
